Tinglysningsafgift

Tinglysningsafgift Denmark – Property Registration Fee and Transfer Tax

Tinglysningsafgift is the fee payable when registering a property right in Denmark's digital land register (Tingbogen), maintained by the Danish Courts Administration. It applies to registrations of ownership (skøde), mortgages (pantebreve), and other encumbrances. It represents one of the main transaction costs in Danish property purchases.

For ownership registrations, the tinglysningsafgift consists of two components: a fixed fee (currently DKK 1,850) plus a variable fee of 0.6% of the purchase price or the most recent public land assessment (offentlig ejendomsvurdering) — whichever is higher. This means buyers cannot reduce the tax by declaring a lower purchase price.

For mortgage registrations, the tinglysningsafgift is a fixed fee plus 1.45% of the mortgage amount. When refinancing, buyers can avoid the 1.45% variable component on the existing mortgage amount if the existing mortgage instrument (pantebrev) is reused rather than replaced — similar to the Swedish pantbrev reuse mechanism.

Tinglysning is done electronically in Denmark through the Tinglysningsretten (Land Registration Court) digital system. Most transactions are processed within 1–3 working days. The buyer's lawyer (advokat) or bank typically handles submission as part of the conveyancing process.

The Tingbogen is publicly accessible at tinglysning.dk. Buyers should review the current Tingbog entry for the property before committing, checking for registered charges (hæftelser), easements (servitutter), and any restrictions on use. A full extract (fuld udskrift) provides all registered information.

⚠ Common MistakeUsing the purchase price as the tax base when the official land assessment is higher. The tinglysningsafgift always applies to the higher of purchase price or assessment — a purchase at below-assessment price still triggers the higher tax base.
💡 Expert TipWhen refinancing, ask your bank whether existing mortgage instruments can be reused to avoid the 1.45% variable component on the existing principal. The potential saving is significant for large mortgages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays tinglysningsafgift?

The buyer pays for ownership registration; the borrower pays for mortgage registration.

Is tinglysningsafgift deductible?

No. It is treated as an acquisition cost added to the cost base for ejendomsavancebeskatning (property capital gains tax purposes).

Does the fee apply to agricultural land?

Yes, the same fee structure applies, though specific rules may apply to certain agricultural land transactions.
