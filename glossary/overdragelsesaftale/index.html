Overdragelsesaftale

Overdragelsesaftale Denmark – Property Transfer Agreement

The overdragelsesaftale (transfer agreement) is the legally binding purchase contract for Danish property sales, typically prepared by the estate agent using a standardised form approved by the Danish Real Estate Council (DE). Signing the overdragelsesaftale commits both buyer and seller to the transaction on the agreed terms.

The overdragelsesaftale contains: the property description (cadastral reference), agreed purchase price, completion date (overtagelsesdagen), included fixtures and fittings, the condition of the property (referencing the tilstandsrapport), any conditions (such as financing), and the allocation of running costs between buyer and seller. It also incorporates by reference the salgsopstilling and tilstandsrapport.

Danish law provides buyers with a statutory withdrawal right (fortrydelsesret) of six working days after signing the overdragelsesaftale. If the buyer exercises this right, they must pay a 1% break fee (godtgørelse) to the seller. This provides limited protection against impulse decisions, though six days is a short window to complete financing arrangements.

After the overdragelsesaftale, the buyer's advokat arranges the mortgage, conducts title searches, and prepares the skøde (deed of conveyance). On completion day (overtagelsesdagen), the skøde is signed, the purchase price is transferred, and the keys are handed over. The advokat then submits the skøde for tinglysning.

The overdragelsesaftale typically contains a pro-rata adjustment (refusionsopgørelse) provision: property taxes, utilities, and other periodic costs are apportioned between buyer and seller as of the overtagelsesdagen. This financial reconciliation is calculated after completion and settled separately.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming the six-day fortrydelsesret gives you time to arrange financing from scratch. It does not — mortgage applications take weeks. Secure at least a conditional offer before signing the overdragelsesaftale.
💡 Expert TipInstruct your advokat before signing the overdragelsesaftale, not after. The advokat needs time to review the contract terms, identify any conditions to be inserted, and advise on the fortrydelsesret deadline.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the overdragelsesaftale the same as the skøde?

No. The overdragelsesaftale is the binding contract. The skøde is the formal deed of conveyance that transfers legal title and is registered in Tingbogen. Both are required.

Can conditions be added to a standardised overdragelsesaftale?

Yes. The standardised form has provision for special conditions (særlige vilkår) where the parties can add financing conditions, survey conditions, or other bespoke terms.

What happens if the seller cannot complete on the overtagelsesdagen?

The buyer can either agree an extension or terminate the contract and claim damages. The specific remedies depend on the contractual terms and the reason for the seller's default.
