Byggetilladelse

Byggetilladelse Denmark – Building Permit Process and Rules

A byggetilladelse (building permit) is the municipal authorisation required in Denmark before commencing construction of new buildings, extensions above certain thresholds, or significant alterations of existing buildings. It is governed by the Danish Building Act (Byggeloven) and the current technical building regulations (BR18, effective from 2018).

Applications are submitted to the local municipality (kommune) via the building application portal (Byg og Miljø, BOM). The application must include architectural and structural drawings, an energy frame calculation, a drainage plan, and documentation that the project complies with the local development plan (lokalplan). Processing takes 8–12 weeks for standard applications.

BR18 introduced performance-based requirements replacing older prescriptive rules. Designers must document compliance with energy requirements, accessibility, fire safety, and indoor climate standards. This creates more flexibility in design but requires more thorough technical documentation.

Upon completion, the builder must notify the municipality (completion notification — færdigmelding), which triggers a final inspection and registration in the Building and Dwelling Register (Bygnings- og Boligregistret, BBR). The BBR records all legal uses, areas, and technical characteristics of Danish buildings and is publicly accessible.

Buildings must be registered in the BBR before they can be mortgaged or sold. Discrepancies between the BBR record and the actual building — such as unlicensed extensions — are a common title issue in Danish property sales. Buyers should compare the BBR extract with the actual building before purchase.

⚠ Common MistakePurchasing a property without checking the BBR extract. Unlicensed rooms, extensions, or changed use that are not registered in BBR represent potential liabilities for the buyer.
💡 Expert TipRequest the full BBR extract from bbr.dk before making an offer. Compare the registered floor area, use, and construction year with what you can observe in the property. Unexplained discrepancies need to be investigated before purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does BBR stand for?

Bygnings- og Boligregistret — the official Danish database of all buildings and dwellings, recording legal use, area, construction year, and technical characteristics.

Is a byggetilladelse required for solar panels in Denmark?

Not typically for residential solar panels within certain size limits. However, panels on protected buildings or in some conservation areas may require both building approval and heritage authority consent.

What happens if I build without a byggetilladelse?

Illegal construction (ulovligt byggeri) can be ordered removed at the owner's expense. The municipality can also charge fees and issue notices requiring regularisation.
