Ejerudgifter (Ownership Costs)

Ejerudgifter are the ongoing ownership costs of a Danish property beyond the mortgage — including property taxes, utilities, owner association fees, and maintenance. Danish property listings must by law disclose these costs.

Danish estate agents are legally required to include an ejerudgifter calculation in property listings. This is a standardised summary of the expected annual ownership costs, covering: ejendomsvaerdiskat (property value tax), grundskyld (land value tax), utilities (el, vand, varme), ejerforening fees (for apartments), and insurance. It allows buyers to compare the true annual cost of living in different properties.

Ejendomsvaerdiskat is a national tax on the value of an owner-occupied property, currently calculated at 0.92% of the assessed value up to a threshold and 3% above it. A reform that took effect from 2024 updated the assessment values to market levels after years of frozen assessments, causing significant changes to tax bills for many owners.

Grundskyld is a municipal land value tax, separately calculated and varying by municipality. It represents a significant proportion of total property taxation for detached houses on large plots.

For andelsboliger (cooperative apartments), ejerudgifter also includes the monthly boligafgift — the cooperative contribution covering the building's shared mortgage, maintenance, and management. This is analogous to the Norwegian fellesgjeld concept and can be substantial.

When comparing a house to an apartment, include not just the monthly mortgage but the full ejerudgifter. An apartment with high ejerforening fees can end up costing more per month than a house with a higher mortgage but low overheads.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Focusing only on the mortgage repayment and ignoring ejerudgifter leads buyers to underestimate true monthly costs by 20–40% in many cases. Always calculate the full monthly housing cost before committing.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Request the seller's most recent ejendomsskat (property tax) assessment and energy bill. These are the two most variable ejerudgifter line items and the ones most worth scrutinising in older properties.

Frequently Asked Questions

What changed in the 2024 property tax reform?

The Danish government updated property value assessments to reflect current market prices, which had been frozen for years. Many properties saw large jumps in assessed value, with corresponding increases in ejendomsvaerdiskat and grundskyld.

Are owner-occupied properties taxed differently from rental properties?

Yes. Ejendomsvaerdiskat only applies to owner-occupied primary residences. Rental properties are taxed differently and the net rental income is subject to income tax.

What is the boligafgift in an andelsbolig?

It is the monthly payment made by each andelsbolig member to the cooperative, covering the building's shared mortgage, maintenance reserve, insurance, and management costs. It is not a market rent — it is determined by the cooperative's costs.
