Tilstandsrapport / Boligsalgsrapport (Condition Survey)

The tilstandsrapport (condition survey) and boligsalgsrapport (housing sales report) are complementary reports prepared by licensed surveyors before a Danish residential property sale. Together they enable sellers to limit liability for hidden defects.

Danish property law gives sellers a powerful tool: if a seller commissions a tilstandsrapport (condition survey) from a licensed beskikket bygningssagkyndig (certified building surveyor) and takes out a ejerskifteforsikring (buyer's warranty insurance), they can limit their future liability for hidden defects discovered after sale. Without these reports, sellers face potentially unlimited liability for concealed faults.

The tilstandsrapport covers the physical condition of the building — foundation, roof, walls, floors, moisture, windows — and rates each element using a damage classification system: UN (no damage), K1 (minor), K2 (significant), K3 (critical), and IB (not assessed). A copy must be provided to the buyer before any binding agreement.

The huseftersynsordning (house inspection scheme), administered by the state, sets the rules for how reports are prepared and which building elements must be covered. It also regulates which defects the seller's ejerskifteforsikring must cover, providing buyers with a guaranteed minimum level of protection.

Alongside the tilstandsrapport, the boligsalgsrapport includes an elinstallationsrapport (electrical installation report) from a licensed electrician, covering the wiring and consumer unit. Defective electrical systems are a common cause of post-sale claims and must be assessed separately.

Buyers should read both reports carefully before signing the purchase agreement (slutseddel). A K3 rating on a structural element means the issue requires immediate attention — factor the cost of repair into your offer price or make the purchase conditional on the seller rectifying it.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming a K2 rating is minor is a mistake. K2 means a significant defect that needs attention — not imminent collapse, but real cost. Get independent cost estimates for every K2 and K3 item before finalising your offer.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If the seller does not provide a tilstandsrapport, they face full liability for hidden defects. In that scenario, commission your own independent survey — the cost is several thousand DKK but trivial relative to the potential liability you are absorbing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the ejerskifteforsikring cover everything in the tilstandsrapport?

No. Defects already noted in the tilstandsrapport are typically excluded from the insurance — they were disclosed, so they are not hidden. The insurance covers defects that were not visible at the time of survey.

Who pays for the tilstandsrapport?

The seller pays for both the tilstandsrapport and the ejerskifteforsikring premium. The cost is typically DKK 5,000–10,000 for the report plus insurance.

Can I waive the right to receive the reports?

Technically yes, but this would mean the seller retains full liability — which few sellers will accept. In practice, almost all Danish residential sales include the tilstandsrapport package.
