Ejendomsmægler

Ejendomsmægler Denmark – Estate Agent Role and Commission

An ejendomsmægler (estate agent) in Denmark is a licensed professional regulated by the Danish Business Authority (Erhvervsstyrelsen) under the Estate Agents Act (Ejendomsmæglerloven). Danish estate agents must hold a specific ejendomsmægler degree and be registered with Erhvervsstyrelsen. Unregistered persons are prohibited from conducting agency activities.

Like Scandinavian counterparts, Danish estate agents have a dual duty to act fairly toward both buyer and seller. They must provide buyers with a salgsopstilling (property particulars sheet) containing standardised information including: assessed values, annual costs, energy label, and any known material defects. The salgsopstilling format is prescribed by law.

Estate agent commission (salær) is paid by the seller and is typically 1.5–2.5% of the sale price plus VAT. In addition, sellers pay for the energy label (energimærke), property valuation report (tilstandsrapport by a building inspector), and advertising. These additional costs can add DKK 20,000–60,000 to the total cost of selling.

The tilstandsrapport (property condition report) prepared by an independent building inspector (byggesagkyndig) is central to the Danish system. If the seller provides both a valid tilstandsrapport and a buyer's warranty insurance offer (ejerskifteforsikring), the seller's liability for hidden defects is significantly limited. Most buyers take out the ejerskifteforsikring as protection.

Buyers are advised by law to appoint their own advokat (property lawyer) to review the purchase documents independently. The estate agent represents the transaction but the advokat represents the buyer's specific interests. Most Danish banks require an advokat's involvement for mortgage transactions.

⚠ Common MistakeNot taking out the ejerskifteforsikring (buyer's warranty insurance) when a valid tilstandsrapport is provided. Without it, you bear the risk of hidden defects that the tilstandsrapport did not identify.
💡 Expert TipRead the tilstandsrapport carefully before bidding, not just before signing. The report grades defects by severity (K0–K3). K3 defects (critical) should be priced into your offer or investigated further before committing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an ejerskifteforsikring?

Buyer's property warranty insurance that covers hidden defects not identified in the tilstandsrapport. The seller is required to offer to pay for at least half the premium as a condition of limiting their defect liability.

How long does Danish property conveyancing take?

Typically four to six weeks from price agreement to completion. The Danish system involves the buyer's advokat reviewing the title, arranging financing, and completing the tinglysning.

Can I make a conditional offer in Denmark?

Yes. Offers can be made conditional on financing (finansieringsforbehold) or satisfactory survey review. The specific conditions and deadlines should be carefully drafted.
