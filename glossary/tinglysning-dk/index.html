Property & Conveyancing

What is Tinglysning (Danish Property Registration)?

Denmark's electronic land registration system — all property transfers, mortgages, and encumbrances are registered in the Tingbogen via tinglysning.dk. Registration duty (tinglysningsafgift) of DKK 1,850 + 0.6% applies.

Key Facts — Tinglysning (Danish Property Registration)
  • Platform: tinglysning.dk (electronic, operated by Domstolsstyrelsen)
  • Key document: skode (deed of transfer)
  • Registration duty: DKK 1,850 + 0.6% of purchase price
  • Mortgage duty: DKK 1,850 + 1.45% of loan amount
  • Processing: typically minutes to hours electronically

Tinglysning is the Danish system for registering rights in real property. All property transfers, mortgages (pantebreve), easements, and other real property rights must be registered in the Tingbogen — Denmark's official land register — through the electronic tinglysning.dk platform operated by the Danish Courts Administration (Domstolsstyrelsen). Registration is constitutive for effectiveness against third parties.

For a property purchase, the key registration document is the skode (deed of transfer). The skode must be prepared — typically by the buyer's lawyer (advokat) or an online conveyancing service — and submitted electronically to tinglysning.dk. The document must include the property's BBR number, the parties' CPR/CVR numbers, the purchase price, and the transfer date.

The tinglysningsafgift for property transfers comprises a fixed fee of DKK 1,850 plus 0.6% of the purchase price (or the public assessment value — ejendomsvaerdi — if higher). For a DKK 4 million property, the variable component is DKK 24,000. Mortgage registrations (pantebreve) attract a separate tinglysningsafgift of DKK 1,850 plus 1.45% of the loan amount.

Denmark's electronic tinglysning system (introduced 2009) is among the most advanced in Europe. Most applications are processed automatically within minutes to hours. The Tingbogen is publicly searchable — any person can verify ownership and encumbrances on any Danish property.

Once tinglysning is complete, the buyer appears in the Tingbogen as the registered owner. A tinglysningsattest (registration certificate) is available to download from tinglysning.dk. Lawyers and banks routinely use Tingbogen searches as part of property and mortgage due diligence.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes use the public assessment value (ejendomsvaerdi) as the basis for tinglysningsafgift calculation, not realising it is assessed on the higher of purchase price and ejendomsvaerdi. If you purchase below the assessed value, you still pay duty on the higher amount.
Expert Tip: Denmark operates a system of "realkredit" mortgage institutions (Realkredit Danmark, Nykredit, etc.) that issue covered bonds backed by property portfolios. Danish mortgages are often fixed-rate callable bonds — a sophisticated system unique to Denmark.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I search the Danish Tingbogen?

Search tinglysning.dk — enter the property address to find the current registered owner, any registered mortgages, and encumbrances. The search is free and public.

Can I do Danish tinglysning myself?

Technically yes — tinglysning.dk allows self-service submissions with MitID. However, drafting a valid skode requires legal knowledge. For significant property purchases, using a Danish advokat is strongly recommended.

What happens if a Danish property transfer is not tinglyst?

An unregistered transfer is valid between buyer and seller but gives no protection against third-party creditors or a subsequent buyer who registers first. Always submit the skode for tinglysning promptly on completion.
