Andelsboligforening

An andelsboligforening is a Danish cooperative housing association in which members own a share (andel) entitling them to occupy a specific dwelling, rather than owning the property itself — a uniquely Danish ownership form distinct from owner-occupation or rental.

The andelsboligforening (cooperative housing association) is a distinctive Danish housing tenure in which the association owns the building and members purchase a andel (share) that gives them the right to occupy a specific apartment. Unlike a ejerlejlighed (owner-occupied apartment), the member does not own their unit — they own a share in the association that owns the building.

The value of an andel is limited by law (the Andelsboligloven). The maximum permitted price is calculated using one of three valuation methods: the property's public assessment value (offentlig ejendomsvurdering), a professional valuation capped by legislation, or the net capitalised rental income. This cap protects affordability but means andelsboliger trade at significant discounts to equivalent ejerlejligheder.

Before buying an andel, the prospective buyer must receive the association's annual accounts, balance sheet, budget, and information about the building's condition and any significant debts. The association's loan obligations are shared among members and affect the monthly boligafgift (the housing charge covering mortgage, maintenance, and administration).

Andelsboliger can only be sold to persons approved by the association's board (bestyrelse). The board may reject buyers who do not meet the association's membership criteria, though arbitrary exclusion is challengeable. Waiting lists (ventelister) for popular associations can be years long.

The andel's value may be reduced if the association has large debts or structural defects. Buyers should carefully review the association's accounts, any pending major renovation, and the ratio of debt to assessed property value. Associations in poor financial health may need to levy special contributions or, in extreme cases, dissolve the cooperative.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes compare andel prices directly with ejerlejlighed prices in the same area without adjusting for the legal price cap. An andel appears cheap because it is legally prevented from reaching full market value — this is a feature of the tenure, not a bargain.

The boligafgift should be stress-tested against potential interest rate increases if the association has variable-rate loans.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can an andel be mortgaged?

Andelsboliger cannot be mortgaged with a realkreditlån (mortgage bond loan) in the same way as ejerlejligheder. Limited bank financing may be available using the andel as security at less favourable rates.

What happens if the andelsboligforening has financial problems?

Members face the risk of higher boligafgift, special levies, or in extreme cases dissolution of the association. Due diligence on the association's finances is therefore as important as inspecting the apartment.

Can I rent out my andel?

Subletting is generally only permitted with the board's approval and is often restricted or subject to time limits under the association's rules.
