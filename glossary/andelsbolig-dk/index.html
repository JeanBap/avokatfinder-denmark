Andelsbolig (Housing Cooperative Share)

An andelsbolig is a Danish housing cooperative share entitling the holder to occupy a specific apartment. It is distinct from freehold ownership, and the share price is regulated by the cooperative's valuation methods.

Andelsboliger are a significant and distinctly Danish housing tenure. Rather than owning the apartment outright (like an ejerlejlighed) or renting from a landlord, the andelsbolig holder owns a share (andel) in the cooperative (andelsboligforening) that owns the building. The share price is regulated by law — specifically the Andelsboligloven — and cannot be set freely at market value.

The maximum permissible share price is calculated using one of three statutory valuation methods: the public property assessment (offentlig vurdering), a professional valuation (valuarvurdering), or the cost of rebuilding (anskaffelsesvaerdi). The method used by the cooperative is set in its vedtaegter (statutes) and cannot be changed without a supermajority vote.

This price regulation means andelsboliger are typically cheaper to buy than equivalent ejerlejligheder in the same neighbourhood. However, it also means the share cannot fully appreciate with the market — the upside is capped by the valuation method.

The cooperative carries shared debt (faellesgaeld) from the original mortgage used to acquire or build the property. Each member's monthly boligafgift services their proportional share of this debt plus operating costs.

Sales of andelsboliger must be approved by the cooperative's board (bestyrelse) and cannot be sold above the maximum permitted price. Selling above the ceiling is illegal and can result in criminal liability. However, permitted "improvements" (forbedringer) made by the departing member — documented, approved kitchen, bathroom upgrades etc. — can be added to the share price up to specific limits.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Paying above the legally permitted andelsbolig price — even by private agreement — is a criminal offence in Denmark. Both buyer and seller can face liability. Confirm the maximum price from the cooperative's administrator before signing anything.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Request the cooperative's most recent regnskab (accounts) and referat (meeting minutes) before bidding. Look specifically at the faellesgaeld level and upcoming renovation plans, which will directly affect your monthly boligafgift.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I mortgage an andelsbolig?

Yes, but with restrictions. Banks offer andelsbolig loans secured against the share. Loan amounts are limited to a percentage of the cooperative's value, and some lenders exclude cooperatives with high faellesgaeld.

What is the difference between an andelsbolig and an ejerlejlighed?

An ejerlejlighed is a freehold apartment — you own the unit and the proportional share of the building outright. An andelsbolig is a cooperative share with price regulation. Ejerlejligheder trade at full market value; andelsboliger are capped.

Can foreigners buy andelsboliger?

Yes. There are no nationality restrictions on andelsbolig purchases in Denmark for EU citizens. Non-EU citizens purchasing property in Denmark may in some cases need permission from the Ministry of Justice, though this primarily applies to holiday homes and agricultural land.
