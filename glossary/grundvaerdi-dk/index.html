Grundværdi

Grundværdi Denmark – Land Value Assessment and Property Tax Base

Grundværdi (land value) is the official assessed value of the land component of a Danish property, set by the Danish Property Assessment Authority (Vurderingsstyrelsen). It is distinct from the total property value (ejendomsværdi) and forms the basis for the annual grundskyld (land value tax), one of two main property taxes in Denmark.

The grundskyld is levied by the municipality at a rate set locally (typically 1.6–3.4% of grundværdi). Unlike the ejendomsværdiskat (which applies to the full property value and is capped), grundskyld applies to the land value alone. This reflects the Danish tax policy tradition of taxing land more heavily than improvements.

Denmark overhauled its property assessment system in 2023–2024, with new assessments replacing the previous system that had been suspended for years due to errors. The new system uses automated valuation models and has resulted in significant assessment changes for many properties. Buyers should obtain the current assessment before purchase.

The ejendomsværdiskat (property value tax on the building and land combined) is a federal tax calculated as 0.51% of the ejendomsværdi up to DKK 9.2 million, and 1.4% above that threshold. This tax applies only to owner-occupied primary and holiday residences — not to investment rental properties, which are taxed differently.

Buyers of investment properties should understand that rental properties are not subject to ejendomsværdiskat but are subject to grundskyld. The income from rental properties is taxed as capital income (kapitalindkomst). The distinction between owner-occupied and investment property tax treatment is fundamental to Danish property investment analysis.

⚠ Common MistakeUsing the suspended pre-reform assessments for tax planning. The 2023–2024 reform introduced new assessments that in many cases differ substantially from the suspended values. Always use the current Vurderingsstyrelsen assessment.
💡 Expert TipFor investment property purchases, model both grundskyld and any renovations' impact on future assessments. Properties with significant improvement potential may see grundværdi increase substantially after renovation, increasing the annual grundskyld.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often are grundværdi assessments updated?

Under the new system, residential properties are assessed annually. Commercial properties are assessed every other year.

Can I appeal a grundværdi assessment?

Yes. Property owners can appeal assessments to Vurderingsstyrelsen and subsequently to the Land Assessment Board (Landsskatteretten).

Does grundskyld apply to agricultural land?

Agricultural land is subject to a reduced grundskyld rate and has its own assessment rules under the property assessment system.
