Arv

Definition: Danish legal term for inheritance of property and assets from a deceased person's estate.

Overview

Arv refers to inheritance in Danish law, encompassing the legal transfer of a deceased person's property and assets to designated heirs. The distribution of an arv is governed by either the deceased's testament or Danish statutory succession rules.

In Denmark, statutory succession establishes a priority order of heirs: spouse, children, parents, and more distant relatives. If no valid will exists, the estate is divided according to this legal hierarchy.

A person has the right to accept or reject an arv within a specified time frame. Accepting an arv means assuming all obligations and debts as well as gaining all assets. Rejection allows one to avoid liability for estate debts.

Danish law provides protections for spouses, who typically receive a significant portion of the arv and may have rights to the marital home regardless of will provisions. Children also have protected inheritance rights.

The value of an arv may be subject to inheritance tax (arveavgift) in Denmark, depending on the amount and the heir's relationship to the deceased. Tax obligations must be settled before distribution of assets.

Key Facts

  • Inheritance of property under Danish law
  • Distributed by will or statutory succession
  • Must be accepted or rejected within time period
  • Subject to potential inheritance tax
  • Spouse and children have protected rights

Common Mistake

Watch out: Accepting an arv without understanding the tax implications or verifying that assets exceed liabilities.

Pro Tip

💡 Consult a Danish tax advisor and lawyer before accepting a large inheritance to understand obligations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to accept or reject an arv?

In Denmark, you typically have 6 months to make this decision, though circumstances may extend this period.

What is arveavgift and who pays it?

Arveavgift is Danish inheritance tax, payable by heirs depending on the inheritance amount and their relationship to the deceased.

Can a spouse inherit everything in Denmark?

A spouse may inherit a significant portion, especially if children also inherit. Danish law balances spouse and child protection.
