Pantegæld

Definition: Danish term for mortgage debt or security interest registered against real property.

Overview

Pantegæld is the Danish term for a mortgage or secured debt registered against real property. When a property owner borrows money, the lender typically requires a pantegæld to secure the loan with the property as collateral.

A pantegæld is registered in the tinglysning (property registry) and creates a legal claim against the property. If the borrower defaults, the lender can initiate foreclosure to sell the property and recover the debt.

Multiple pantegælds can be registered against the same property, each with a priority ranking based on registration date. First-registered mortgages have priority in receiving payment from sale proceeds.

The amount of pantegæld is limited by law and cannot exceed the assessed value of the property. Lenders in Denmark are subject to strict regulations regarding pantegæld to protect borrowers from over-leveraging.

Once a pantegæld is paid off, the lender must formally discharge it from the registry. Until discharged, the lender retains a legal claim against the property, even if the debt is actually satisfied.

Key Facts

  • Mortgage or security interest on Danish property
  • Registered in tinglysning (property registry)
  • Creates priority claims if property foreclosed
  • Amount limited to property value
  • Must be formally discharged when paid

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not understanding the full terms of pantegæld, including interest rates, payment schedules, and default consequences.

Pro Tip

💡 Review pantegæld documents carefully with a Danish lawyer before signing, especially regarding foreclosure terms.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many pantegælds can I have on one property?

Multiple mortgages are permitted, ranked by priority based on registration order.

What happens if I cannot pay my pantegæld?

The lender may initiate foreclosure, selling the property to recover the debt.

How do I remove a pantegæld when the loan is paid?

Request the lender formally discharge (slet) the mortgage from the tinglysning registry.
