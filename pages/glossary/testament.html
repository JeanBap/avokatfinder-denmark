Testament

Definition: A Danish will that specifies how a person's estate should be distributed after death.

Overview

A testament in Danish law is a formal legal document in which a person declares how their property should be distributed upon death. It must be created with legal capacity and testamentary intent, and must comply with strict formal requirements.

Danish law recognizes handwritten wills (håndskrevne testamenter) and formal wills witnessed by two witnesses (vidner). Handwritten wills must be dated and signed by the testator; witnessed wills require additional formalities.

For a testament to be valid, it must clearly identify the testator, specify the distribution of assets, and be properly signed and dated. Witnessed wills must be signed in the presence of both witnesses, who attest to the testator's capacity.

A testament can designate an executor (testamentshåndfæster) to manage the estate and execute the testator's wishes. It can include specific bequests, charitable donations, and conditional gifts.

Danish law protects certain heirs through mandatory share (arvelod) provisions, ensuring that spouses and children receive a minimum inheritance regardless of will provisions. These protections cannot be entirely bypassed.

Key Facts

  • Formal document declaring asset distribution
  • Can be handwritten or formally witnessed
  • Must be dated and signed by testator
  • Can designate executor and include conditions
  • Subject to mandatory share protections

Common Mistake

Watch out: Creating an informal testament that fails to meet Danish formality requirements, risking invalidity and disputes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the formal requirements for a valid Danish testament?

Must be dated, signed by the testator, and either handwritten or witnessed by two independent adults with capacity.

Can I modify my testament?

Yes, you can create a new testament or a supplement (tillæg) to modify an existing will at any time.

What is an arvelod and how does it affect my testament?

Arvelod is the mandatory inheritance share for spouses and children; you cannot entirely disinherit them.
